Media & Influencer Coverage - Typhoon Karding

ABS-CBN coverage and influencer mentions

GMA coverage and influencer mentions

Chart Description:

The data from both ABS-CBN and GMA during Typhoon Karding underscores the critical role of media coverage on social media in shaping public awareness, emotional response, and real-time action during natural disasters. The contrast in engagement levels between the two networks—ABS-CBN's tweets averaging hundreds to thousands of reposts and likes, versus GMA’s typically lower double-digit figures—reveals how content framing, timing, and platform resonance impact audience behavior.

ABS-CBN’s tweets, particularly those providing urgent warnings—such as signal advisories and expected impact zones—and emotional appeals like prayers or community aid, saw substantial amplification through reposts and high like counts. For instance, one tweet warning about “catastrophic effects” received nearly 3,000 likes and 1,388 reposts, indicating a strong public impulse to share actionable and emotional content. These high-engagement posts demonstrate that effective disaster communication on social media must be immediate, emotionally resonant, and easily shareable.

Meanwhile, GMA’s content—although similarly informative—generally garnered less engagement. Their updates focused more on formal announcements and institutional efforts, which, while important, appeared less emotionally engaging or urgent in tone. Posts highlighting government coordination or late-stage weather developments attracted minimal interaction. This difference suggests that audiences on platforms like Twitter are more responsive to content that feels directly relevant to personal safety or community welfare, rather than institutional updates.

From a broader perspective, this analysis highlights the vital importance of media organizations on social media as both information disseminators and emotional anchors during crises. High engagement can help ensure that critical updates reach wider audiences quickly, enabling faster preparation, response, and even recovery. Social media dynamics—especially the speed and scale of user amplification—can turn a single tweet into a life-saving alert or a rallying point for aid. In sum, the effectiveness of media coverage in disasters is deeply tied to how well it leverages social media's strengths: timeliness, emotional engagement, clarity, and shareability. The ABS-CBN and GMA data not only reflect differing levels of engagement but also reveal how media organizations must continuously adapt their digital strategies to ensure that life-saving information truly reaches—and moves—the public.

Based on the numbers in the dataset, the engagement metrics—reposts, likes, and replies—reveal how users responded to different types of tweets during Super Typhoon Karding. Other tweets had minimal replies, ranging from 2 to 21, suggesting that news-style bulletins are typically not conversational but rather informative. Overall, the data shows that the public is most responsive—in both amplification and emotional engagement—to tweets that are either highly urgent (disaster warnings) or deeply resonant (prayers, calls for help), with the numbers reflecting distinct user behaviors depending on the content’s nature.

GMA Engagement Data Analysis

The GMA Network dataset reflects a significantly lower level of engagement compared to the ABS-CBN dataset, based on the numerical data for reposts, likes, and replies. The most engaged tweet in this set—Tweet 2, reporting that President Marcos Jr. was in contact with Cabinet officials—garnered only 56 reposts, 174 likes, and 11 replies, suggesting moderate public interest in leadership updates. Tweets invoking prayer or offering emotional appeals (like Tweet 1) had slightly better performance with 23 reposts and 90 likes, indicating that faith-based messaging had some traction, though still limited. In general, most tweets across the dataset received fewer than 25 reposts, under 100 likes, and 1 or no replies, with several tweets (e.g., Tweets 8–13) having virtually no engagement at all. Tweets about weather updates post-landfall, which typically included technical details, were the least engaged, often receiving 0–1 reposts or likes. This suggests a decline in public interest once the peak of the typhoon had passed or that these updates lacked urgency or emotional appeal. Overall, the data shows that while GMA disseminated timely and relevant updates, the posts were less amplified and interacted with by users. This could reflect differences in audience behavior, post content style, or platform strategy. Numerically, the lower engagement suggests that users were either less compelled to share or emotionally invest in GMA’s posts or were more focused on other sources, such as ABS-CBN, for information and expression during Typhoon Karding.

Community support and volunteer activities for Typhoon Henry

Typhoon Henry Data Description:

The data reveals a noticeable difference in the volume and nature of social media posts related to Typhoon Henry between the community and the government. The community’s posts, totaling 10 tweets, focus largely on grassroots relief efforts, volunteer coordination, urgent calls for donations, and sharing emergency contact information. These posts are characterized by personal appeals and updates on localized aid activities, reflecting a bottom-up response driven by individuals, volunteer groups, and small organizations aiming to provide immediate support to affected communities. The government’s posts, on the other hand, also number 10 but tend to emphasize official disaster response mechanisms, mobilization of resources, coordinated relief efforts by government agencies, and public service announcements. Government posts generally have higher engagement metrics such as likes and reposts, which indicates broader visibility and reach, possibly due to official accounts and their established followership. Overall, while both sectors are active, the community’s social media activity is more informal, urgent, and focused on mobilizing help directly on the ground, whereas the government’s messaging aims to inform the public about ongoing organized response efforts and institutional readiness. This comparison highlights the complementary roles of community-driven initiatives and official government responses in managing disaster relief — the community acts as immediate responders and communicators of urgent needs, while the government coordinates large-scale resources and provides authoritative information to the public. This dynamic interplay is crucial in enhancing disaster resilience and ensuring timely aid reaches those affected by Typhoon Henry.

This section provides a rich dataset for SMD by illustrating how social media facilitates collective action and community mobilization during disasters. The distinct characteristics of community posts (grassroots, urgent, personal appeals) versus government posts (official, resource-focused, broader announcements) highlight different communication strategies and network behaviors. The community's focus on immediate, localized needs demonstrates the power of social media in enabling rapid, decentralized responses. The observation that government posts have higher engagement despite the community's direct action focus is significant for SMD; it may point to the established reach and network centrality of official accounts, or a public tendency to engage with authoritative information sources even while participating in grassroots efforts. This data is relevant for understanding the symbiotic relationship between formal and informal response networks, the flow of information and resources between them, and how social media platforms serve as a crucial interface for coordinating diverse efforts in disaster management.

GMA & ABS-CBN Data Comparison:

In comparing the social media engagement of ABS-CBN and GMA on posts related to Typhoon Henry (#HenryPH), ABS-CBN demonstrated a significantly higher overall interaction due to its more consistent and frequent updates. ABS-CBN posted three separate tweets about the typhoon, accumulating a total of 998 likes, 388 reposts, and 72 replies. In contrast, GMA had only one tweet regarding Typhoon Henry, which gathered 589 likes, 214 reposts, and 50 replies. Interestingly, both networks posted the exact same bulletin on August 31, 2022—announcing the entry of Super Typhoon Hinnamnor into the Philippine Area of Responsibility under the local name “Henry”—and both received the same amount of reposts (214) and replies (50) on that specific post, suggesting a high level of audience engagement with this particular update regardless of source. Despite this equal performance on the overlapping post, ABS-CBN’s additional tweets provided more timely updates, allowing them to gain an edge in total engagement. One of ABS-CBN’s earlier posts from 2018 gathered 171 likes, 75 reposts, and 2 replies, while a follow-up bulletin in September 2022 added another 238 likes, 99 reposts, and 20 replies to their overall numbers. These figures show that ABS-CBN’s strategy of posting multiple times during the course of the typhoon’s development helped them generate 409 more likes, 174 more reposts, and 22 more replies than GMA overall. In summary, while GMA’s single post was strong in performance, ABS-CBN’s broader coverage and frequent updates on Typhoon Henry led to higher total engagement. This highlights the value of consistent, real-time reporting during major weather events, as it helps media organizations reach and inform a larger audience.

This comparative analysis is central to SMD, particularly in understanding media effects, agenda-setting, and audience engagement on social platforms during crises. The higher engagement for ABS-CBN, linked to update frequency, illustrates the importance of sustained information flow in maintaining audience attention and perceived relevance. The identical engagement on the overlapping critical bulletin suggests that for high-stakes information, the message itself can temporarily override source preference, a key finding for crisis communication. However, the overall higher engagement for ABS-CBN points to the impact of consistent presence in building a more engaged audience network over time. This data is relevant for studying how media outlets compete for attention and disseminate information in a crowded digital landscape, and how content strategies (e.g., frequency, timeliness) influence their effectiveness and reach within their respective social media ecosystems.
